There is often speculation about the legal standing of e cigarettes when it comes to multiple countries like Nepal, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh etc. Up until recently there was confusion regarding the legal status of vaping in India as well – until in September 2019 when the country decided to ban the category via issuing an ordinance, and then passing the act in both the houses of the parliament. Until then the health officials in India often harassed those in the business of vaping by trying to impose non-legal restrictions stating that the category is already banned. The reality was that if some product is not illegal as per law, it is considered legal. E cigarettes, since they did not contain tobacco, were not classified in the category and act for cigarettes, while they were also not a ‘drug’ as per the per the Drugs & Cosmetics Act. Thus a new act had to be formed which is an elaborate process (and speculated to have been pushed by ITC – a tobacco giant in India in which the government hold indirectly some 30% stake). Similarly, there is currently no act under which e cigarettes or vapes can be categorised in Bangladesh so the statement that thy are illegal here is invalid to begin with. The correct status of e cigarettes in Bangladesh is that they are unregulated and legal. This is the same status that India had before the country wide ban, and the status which currently Nepal also has.
